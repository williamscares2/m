Chicken farming is a popular and profitable venture in Nabumali Town. It's a great way to provide fresh, organic eggs for your family and even generate extra income. Here's a simple guide to help you care for your flock:


1. Choose the Right Breed:
 Layer Breeds:
These breeds are known for their egg-laying abilities. Popular choices include Rhode Island Reds, Leghorns, and Plymouth Rocks. Dual-Purpose Breeds:
These breeds are good for both egg production and meat. Consider breeds like Sussex or Orpingtons.
2. Provide a Safe and Comfortable Coop:
Build a Secure Coop:
Ensure your coop is predator-proof, with sturdy walls and a strong roof. Ventilation:
Good ventilation is essential to prevent diseases. Nesting Boxes:
Provide enough nesting boxes for your hens to lay eggs in peace. Perches:
Offer perches for your chickens to roost on at night.
3. Maintain Cleanliness:
Regular Cleaning:
Clean the coop and nesting boxes regularly to prevent the spread of diseases. Fresh Bedding:
Use clean bedding materials like straw or wood shavings. Water and Feeders:
Keep water and feed containers clean to avoid contamination.
4. Feed a Balanced Diet:
Commercial Feed:
A good quality commercial feed provides essential nutrients. Supplements:
Consider adding supplements like grit and oyster shells for calcium. Fresh Greens:
Offer fresh greens like lettuce or vegetable scraps as a treat.
5. Prevent Diseases:
Vaccination:
Consult with a veterinarian to determine necessary vaccinations. Quarantine New Birds:
Isolate new birds for a few weeks to prevent the spread of diseases. Monitor Health:
Regularly check your chickens for signs of illness, such as lethargy or loss of appetite.
6. Protect from Predators
Secure Coop:
Ensure your coop is predator-proof. Predator Deterrents: Use deterrents like scarecrows or predator-proof fencing.
7. Provide Water:
Fresh Water:
Always provide fresh, clean water. Waterers:
Use waterers that are easy to clean and refill.
Additional Tips:
Handle Gently:
Handle your chickens gently to avoid stress. Regular Inspections:
Check your chickens daily for any signs of illness or injury. Socialize Your Chickens:
Spend time with your chickens to build a bond. Local Knowledge:
Consult with experienced local farmers for specific advice tailored to your region.
By following these guidelines, you can ensure the health and well-being of your chickens and enjoy the benefits of homegrown eggs.
